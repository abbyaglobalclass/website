Java, Indonesia: Climate Justice, Community Resilience & Sustainable Development

An immersive 10–21 day field study for high school and university students, engaging directly with communities on the frontlines of climate change in Yogyakarta, Java.

Sample 10-day itinerary
Orientation, welcome dinner, and introduction to program themes and learning goals.
Cultural orientation, history, politics, and development walking tour of the city.
Explore eco-architecture, local food systems, and social enterprise at Yabbiekayu Eco-Bungalows in Tembi Village.
Workshops with local artisans and discussion of sustainable livelihoods and community-based tourism initiatives.
Community visits focused on water scarcity, farming under climate stress, and locally led adaptation strategies.
Interviews, photography, storytelling, and reflection on gendered climate impacts and community resilience.
Students organize notes, photos, interviews, and inquiry questions into their final project frameworks.
Explore disaster preparedness, local knowledge systems, and community resilience around the Merapi volcano region.
Students develop final projects — photo essays, podcasts, presentations, or policy recommendations.
Students share their learning and reflect on responsibility, justice, and action as global citizens.

Learning from communities, not just about them

AGC programs are designed to help students listen to and learn directly from the people most affected by the issues they study. Through fieldwork, conversations, and reflection, abstract global issues become human, personal, and meaningful.

Students are encouraged to move beyond charity-based thinking and recognize communities as knowledge holders, leaders, and agents of change.
