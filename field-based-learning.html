Our approach
"All field studies are developed in close collaboration with local communities."

Community members participate as teachers and knowledge holders — not simply as hosts. Participants learn directly from artists, farmers, academics, activists, and local leaders, engaging with local histories, livelihoods, cultural traditions, and environmental practices.

Students are encouraged to move beyond simplistic narratives and recognize communities as knowledge holders, leaders, and agents of change.

What students explore

Program Themes.

01

Climate Justice & Adaptation

How communities experience and respond to climate change — water scarcity, agricultural disruption, disaster risk, and locally led resilience strategies.

02

Sustainable Development

How communities balance economic growth, cultural preservation, and environmental sustainability across urban, rural, and coastal environments.

03

Human Rights & Justice

Connecting international human rights frameworks to the real experiences of people working for dignity, equity, and accountability in their communities.

04

Community & Livelihoods

Understanding local knowledge systems, cultural traditions, and how communities sustain themselves — from farmers and artisans to activists and entrepreneurs.

Sample itinerary

Ten Days in Java.

A sample 10-day program — all AGC field studies are fully customizable for your school, curriculum, and learning goals.

Day 1
Arrival in Yogyakarta
→
Orientation, welcome dinner, and introduction to program themes and learning goals.
Day 2
Yogyakarta — history, politics & development
→
Cultural orientation and guided walking tour exploring the city's history, urban development, and political landscape.
Day 3
Yabbiekayu & sustainable design
→
Explore eco-architecture, local food systems, and social enterprise at Yabbiekayu Eco-Bungalows in Tembi Village.
Day 4
Tembi village & community-based tourism
→
Workshops with local artisans, batik makers, and community leaders on sustainable livelihoods and community-based tourism.
Day 5
Gunungkidul — climate adaptation
→
Community visits focused on water scarcity, farming under climate stress, and locally led adaptation strategies in the karst highlands.
Day 6
Women farmers & climate justice
→
Interviews, photography, and storytelling focused on gendered climate impacts and the leadership of women in community resilience.
Day 7
Reflection & project development
→
Students organize fieldwork notes, interview recordings, and inquiry questions into their final project frameworks.
Day 8
Merapi & disaster risk
→
Explore disaster preparedness, local knowledge systems, and community resilience in the shadow of an active volcano.
Day 9
Student project workshop
→
Students develop their final projects — photo essays, podcasts, short documentaries, oral histories, or policy briefs.
Day 10
Final presentations & closing
→
Students share their learning with the group and reflect on responsibility, justice, and action as global citizens.
Community partners
Students learn from — not just about — the communities they visit.

Farmers, artisans, activists, academics, and local leaders all participate as knowledge holders. These relationships are built on respect, reciprocity, and a genuine commitment to learning that benefits both visitors and host communities.
