"FarmHer Stories"

Water scarcity and climate adaptation in Gunungkidul, Indonesia

With support from the Earth Rising Foundation, A Global Classroom is partnering with communities in Gunungkidul Regency to develop a place-based curriculum focused on climate vulnerability, adaptation, and justice. Gunungkidul sits within a karst limestone landscape where rainwater drains rapidly underground, leaving many communities with limited access to surface water despite seasonal rainfall.

Women farmers are often bearing the brunt of these impacts — facing delayed rains, failed crops, mounting debt, and the burden of securing water for their households. At the same time, communities are responding through locally led adaptation strategies: rainwater harvesting, water-sharing networks, community greenhouses, and sustainable tourism initiatives.

"FarmHer Stories" invites students to explore climate change as both an environmental and a justice issue — integrating personal narrative, photography, climate science, human rights, and political economy.

Nusantara Project

A curriculum on Indigenous land rights and the "sustainable" city

With support from the Earth Rising Foundation, AGC is collaborating with Indigenous communities and local partners in Kalimantan and Jakarta to develop an interdisciplinary curriculum centered on Indonesia's new capital city, Nusantara, as a case study in land rights, environmental change, and climate justice.

Kalimantan is home to vast tropical rainforests and Indigenous Dayak communities whose land stewardship practices have shaped the region for generations. While the relocation of Indonesia's capital has been framed as a climate and sustainability solution, it raises critical questions about customary land rights, consent, displacement, and political inclusion.

Through interviews, photography, and video, the project will produce learning materials that encourage students to critically analyze competing narratives of "green development" and consider whose voices are prioritized in national and global climate debates.
