About us

Justice-Centered Education, Rooted in Community.

A Global Classroom develops justice-centered curriculum for the classroom and immersive learning experiences rooted in human rights and sustainable development.

We create inquiry-driven resources exploring global politics, climate justice, inequality, international relations, peace and conflict, and human rights through real-world case studies, relevant concepts, and diverse perspectives.

What drives us

Learning Grounded in Real Communities.

Beyond the classroom, we partner with communities to design field studies, experiential learning opportunities, and place-based educational experiences that connect students and teachers directly to the people and places most affected by the issues they study.

We work with teachers, schools, and organizations to create meaningful educational experiences that connect classrooms to the world — from New York to Indonesia and beyond.

Community members are approached not as passive recipients of help, but as individuals and communities with knowledge, resilience, creativity, and important perspectives to share. Our goal is to create learning experiences rooted in listening, reflection, humility, and genuine human connection.

Our approach
"We connect classrooms to communities — not just as places to visit, but as places to learn from."

Community members participate as teachers and knowledge holders. Participants learn directly from artists, farmers, academics, activists, and local leaders — engaging with local histories, livelihoods, cultural traditions, and environmental practices.
